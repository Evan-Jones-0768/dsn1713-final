Dominaria United Standard Guide

Overview of Dominaria United

Dominaria United, released in September 2022, marked a return to the iconic plane of Dominaria. With its mix of nostalgic callbacks and powerful new cards, the set introduced fresh archetypes and invigorated existing strategies in Standard. The set also reintroduced Domain as a mechanic and included powerful multicolor legends, which played a significant role in shaping the meta.

Key themes in Dominaria United:

  • Domain: Rewards decks that play multiple basic land types.
  • Legendary Matters: Many cards synergize with having legendary permanents on the battlefield.
  • Kicker: Flexible spells that allow for additional effects if extra mana is paid.

Dominaria United Standard Archetypes

1. Esper Midrange
  • Core Cards:
    • Raffine, Scheming Seer
    • Sheoldred, the Apocalypse
    • Wedding Announcement
  • Playstyle:This deck controls the board with efficient removal and creatures that generated value. Raffine enabled card filtering and grew threats, while Sheoldred punished opponents for drawing cards.
  • Strengths: Consistent value generation, excellent removal options, and the ability to adapt to aggro or control matchups.
  • Weaknesses: Vulnerable to faster aggro decks if it can't stabilize quickly.
2. Mono-Black Control
  • Core Cards:
    • Liliana of the Veil
    • Evolved Sleeper
    • Invoke Despair
  • Playstyle:This deck thrives on removing threats and grinding out the opponent with recurring card advantage and heavy hitters.
  • Strengths: Efficient removal suite (Cut Down, Infernal Grasp), card draw, and inevitability.
  • Weaknesses: Struggles against wide boards and enchantment-heavy decks.
3. Grixis Vampires
  • Core Cards:
    • Corpse Appraiser
    • Bloodtithe Harvester
    • Fable of the Mirror-Breaker
  • Playstyle: Leveraging synergistic creatures and card filtering, Grixis Vampires excels at generating incremental value and pressuring opponents.
  • Strengths: Flexible game plan, strong removal, and efficient threats.
  • Weaknesses: Weak to aggressive decks that outpaces its removal.
4. Five-Color Domain Ramp
  • Core Cards:
    • Leyline Binding
    • Herd Migration
    • The Kami War
  • Playstyle: Focused on maximizing the Domain mechanic, this deck ramps into powerful late-game threats while stalling with removal.
  • Strengths: Unrivaled late-game power and flexibility.
  • Weaknesses: Weak early-game defenses against aggro.
5. Gruul Aggro
  • Core Cards:
    • Thundering Raiju
    • Squee, Dubious Monarch
    • Keldon Flamesage
  • Playstyle: Fast-paced and aggressive, this deck aimed to overwhelm opponents with efficient creatures and burn spells.
  • Strengths: Speed and raw damage output.
  • Weaknesses: Vulnerable to sweepers and decks with lifegain.

Key Cards of Dominaria United in Standard

1. Sheoldred, the Apocalypse

Sheoldred quickly became a meta-defining card due to its devastating synergy with card draw. Its ability to punish opponents while stabilizing your life total makes it a staple in many midrange and control decks.

2. Leyline Binding

This enchantment, costing just one mana with Domain active, offers unparalleled removal versatility. It fits seamlessly into multicolor decks and defines the Five-Color Domain archetype.

3. Liliana of the Veil

Returning to Standard, Liliana adds a powerful planeswalker option for midrange decks. Her discard and removal abilities allow her to shine in Mono-Black Control and other slower strategies.

4. Fable of the Mirror-Breaker

A three-mana enchantment that generated value over multiple turns, Fable in instrumental in decks like Grixis Vampires and Rakdos Midrange.

Standard Meta Dynamics

Dominant Decks

Esper Midrange and Mono-Black Control emerged as the frontrunners during the early Dominaria United meta due to their ability to adapt to various matchups. These decks offered a blend of resilience, powerful threats, and flexible removal.

Tips for Navigating the Dominaria United Meta

1. Understand the Matchups:

  • Against midrange, prioritize efficient removal and resilient threats.
  • Against control, apply consistent pressure and include cards like Duress in the sideboard.
  • Against aggro, prioritize lifegain and board wipes.

2. Leverage Sideboards:

  • Include flexible answers like Farewell or Riveteers Charm.
  • Adapt to specific threats in your local meta.

3. Stay Flexible:

  • The meta evolved rapidly as players found new strategies. Keeping up-to-date with decklists and tournament results was crucial.
